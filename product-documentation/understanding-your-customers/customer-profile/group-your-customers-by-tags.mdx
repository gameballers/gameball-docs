---
title: "What Are Group Your Customers by Tags?"
description: "Use tags to manually group customers and customize special rewards and communication campaigns to be sent only to your tagged customers."
---

Tags let you group your customers manually. They are great for highlighting VIP customers who may need VIP rewards, loyal customers who should be treated differently, or when you need to simulate different rewards based on the assigned tags. For example, if you want to create and send communication campaigns, notifications, or assign reward campaigns to only a specific group of customers, tags are a great way to link between these customers and the assigned notifications, emails, or reward campaigns.

<Note>
The maximum number of tags that can be created is **15 tags**.
</Note>

## How to Create Customer Tags

<Note>
The following steps are also what you need to completely remove or delete a tag.
</Note>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Customer Tags">
    On your Gameball dashboard, go to **Settings > Admin Settings > Customer Tags**.

    <Frame>
      ![Customer tags settings page](/images/product-docs/understanding-your-customers/customer-tags-settings.png)
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a new tag">
    On the top right of the page, click **Create New Tag**.

    <Frame>
      ![Create new tag button](/images/product-docs/understanding-your-customers/create-new-tag-button.png)
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure and save the tag">
    Add the tag **name** and choose the tag **color**, then click **Create**.

    <Frame>
      ![Tag name and color configuration](/images/product-docs/understanding-your-customers/tag-name-color-config.png)
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How to Tag Your Customers

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Manual Tagging (Single)">
    <Note>
    The following steps are also what you need to remove tags from a customer.
    </Note>

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open a customer profile">
        On your Gameball dashboard, go to **Customers**, then open any customer page.

        <Frame>
          ![Customer list page](/images/product-docs/understanding-your-customers/customer-list-page.png)
        </Frame>
      </Step>
      <Step title="Add a tag">
        Under the **Segments** section, beside **Tags**, click on **+Add**.

        <Frame>
          ![Add tag to customer](/images/product-docs/understanding-your-customers/add-tag-to-customer.png)
        </Frame>
      </Step>
      <Step title="Select the tag">
        Search for the available tags and assign the tag to the customer.

        <Info>
        This is also where you remove a tag from a customer.
        </Info>
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Bulk Tagging (Group)">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Go to the Customers page">
        On your Gameball dashboard, go to **Customers**.

        <Frame>
          ![Customers page](/images/product-docs/understanding-your-customers/customers-page-bulk.png)
        </Frame>
      </Step>
      <Step title="Apply filters">
        Click on the **Filter Icon**.

        <Frame>
          ![Filter icon](/images/product-docs/understanding-your-customers/filter-icon-bulk-tag.png)
        </Frame>

        Filter your customers according to your needs, then click **More**.

        <Frame>
          ![More actions menu](/images/product-docs/understanding-your-customers/more-actions-bulk-tag.png)
        </Frame>
      </Step>
      <Step title="Add or remove tags">
        Choose between adding or removing tags, then click on **+Add**.

        <Frame>
          ![Add or remove tags option](/images/product-docs/understanding-your-customers/add-remove-tags-option.png)
        </Frame>
      </Step>
      <Step title="Select and apply tags">
        Scroll down and pick the desired tag(s), then click **Apply**.

        All customers in the currently active filter will be tagged with the selected tags.

        <Warning>
        If no filter is applied, the operation will automatically tag all customers on Gameball at the moment of the operation.
        </Warning>
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="API Tagging">
    You can also tag customers using Gameball APIs. Check our [technical documentation](https://developer.gameball.co/rest-api/customers/customer-tags) for more details.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
